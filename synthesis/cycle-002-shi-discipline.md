# Cycle 2 — The Shi Discipline: Read-Mode Classification & Configurational Reads

**Status:** **VALIDATED (2026-06-19).** Piloted, then applied across the full Cycle 2 sweep
(129 chains, Briefings 031–060): read-mode predicts break severity 3/3 slices with no
exceptions — every Inverted/Held-Spurious outcome was R or H; zero O-reads inverted or failed-Z.
Folded into the canonical `CONTINGENCY_AUDIT.md` (§5.11, §6.6) and the daily briefing QC
(`CLAUDE.md`, Stage 1d). Full result: `cycle-002.yaml`; readable audit: `cycle-002.html`.

**Why now:** Cycle 2 is due (Briefing 060 published 2026-06-19). The existing audit protocol
is rigorously *anti-prediction* — it rejects hit-rate scoring as "a category error under
Knightian uncertainty" and asks only *how chains break*. That is the correct negative
discipline. What it lacks is the **positive** counterpart: a named way of reading in-process
structural trends that honors irreducible dynamism *while still interpreting*. That is what
the shi discipline supplies, and it is the empirical wing of the **orienting-vs-knowledge-
representation** theory of strategic/entrepreneurial decision-making.

---

## 1. The axis: orienting vs. knowledge representation

The theory's grounding is already in hand, in the **bandwidth-rotation** argument
(`escaping-the-trap/THEORETICAL_ARG_BANDWIDTH_ROTATION.md`):

> "agents are correctly trained to optimize *representation within bandwidth* … the shift
> cannot be predictable — predictability collapses the dynamic into a *representation-search
> problem* … the orientation move is empty unless it produces *translation*, not just slower
> processing of the same content."

Mapped onto the briefing's reading practice:

| | **Knowledge Representation (R)** | **Orienting (O)** |
|---|---|---|
| Object | An accurate *map* of a fixed structure | The *disposition* of a live configuration |
| Operation | Inference over the model; project it forward | Detect the propensity already at work; position to ride it |
| Claim form | "X will happen" / a single vector | "The field leans toward Y; release paths are {Y₁, Y₂}" |
| Valid when | The world is stable enough to represent | The world is in genuine flux (the briefing's actual condition) |
| Failure | The map dissolves; a represented past is asserted as present fact | Timing/path is misjudged (direction usually holds) |

The briefing operates in a permanently dynamic field, so **orienting is the load-bearing
mode and representation is the trap.** The bandwidth-rotation result is exact here:
over-investing in representation-refinement is precisely the briefing's recurring failure
(the cross-year *template projection* of Briefings 037–039 is the paradigm case — a 2025
*representation* asserted as a 2026 *fact*). The factual-verification discipline patched the
symptom; the shi discipline names the cure: **read the present configuration's own lean;
never project a represented template forward as fact.**

This is not philosophy in the briefing's idiom. It is a reading stance with a checklist.

## 2. Read-Mode Classification (new audit lens)

A third lens, parallel to — not a replacement for — the existing two:

- **Break-Type (Type I–VII)** classifies *what failed in the world*.
- **LLM Signature (S1–S7)** classifies *what failed in the model that generated the chain*.
- **Read-Mode (O / R / H)** classifies *the reading stance the chain took* — independent of
  whether it held.

Every inference chain (and every load-bearing structural read in the eight lenses) is tagged:

- **O — Orienting read.** Names a configuration and its *disposition*; conditional;
  propensity-framed; ideally names ≥2 release paths. Does not assert the event.
  *Signature in the text:* "the field leans / is set to break / is disposed toward…"
- **R — Representation read.** Asserts a fixed structure or single outcome as fact; projects
  a model or a prior template forward. *Signature in the text:* "X will…", a single Z with no
  alternative path, or an analogue-template (a prior year's event) carried as present fact.
- **H — Hybrid.** Names the configuration well (orienting) but **collapses to a single vector
  at the Z-step** (representation). The most common and most diagnostic class: the analysis is
  sound and the conclusion over-commits.

**Central hypothesis (to test in the full sweep):** *Read-mode predicts break severity.*
R-reads concentrate in the worst outcome classes (Inverted, Failed-Z, Held-Spurious) and the
worst signatures (S3 plausibility-mass, S5 categorical stickiness, S2 narrative-over-physical);
O-reads, when they break, break gently (Type-V velocity/path, direction intact). If this holds,
the single highest-value recalibration is mechanical: **convert R-reads and the R-tail of
H-reads into O-reads** — the bandwidth-rotation move applied to analytical writing.

Mapping to the existing taxonomy (prediction for the sweep):

- R-read ⇒ {Type-I agentic-novelty surprise, Type-VI categorical drift, Type-VII spurious hit} × {S3, S5, S7}
- H-read ⇒ {Type-V velocity} × {S2 narrative-over-physical} — the "clean story, single vector" failure
- O-read ⇒ Type-V at worst, often Held / Held-at-Y; the structure was read, only the clock was off

## 3. The Shi-Read Protocol (the discipline)

Five configurational moves and two rules. Pragmatic, not philosophical — each is a question
the read must answer in plain language. The structural vocabulary already supplies move 1;
the discipline is moves 2–5 and the two rules.

**1. 形 — Configuration.** *Name the arrangement, not the outcome.* Which structural
pattern(s) is this? (The 42-pattern vocabulary is the propensity-type library; the citation
power-law — Commons Enclosure 55, Channel Decomposition 23, Keystone Removal 19, … long tail
of hapaxes — is the empirical record of which "shi-types" are real because they recur.)

**2. 勢 — Disposition (the lean).** *Which way is the configuration set to break?* State it as
a tendency, not a forecast. This is the Inference Engine, re-read as propensity.

**3. 節 — Ripeness / timing (the drawn crossbow).** *How loaded is the propensity, and is its
release near or far?* A configuration at maximal stored tension (the "untested instrument,"
the "verdict of the first test," the San Andreas system at a 1,000-year stress high) is a
drawn crossbow; one with slack is not. **This is the move the briefing most often skips, and
it is the source of most velocity (Type-V) breaks.**

**4. 變 — Transformation.** *What does this configuration become next?* Read the transition,
not the static state. The 058→059→060 arc (untested → tested → selectively-executing) is a
single shi-transformation read across three days; tracking transformations is the antidote to
corridor over-fit (reading the same static configuration repeatedly).

**5. Positional read (蓄勢 — stored leverage).** *Where does the configuration confer
leverage, and to whom?* This is the Wise Action section, sharpened: not "what should a founder
do" but "where is the actor positioned to ride the propensity *whichever way it releases*."

**Rule A — Field, not vector.** A disposition is a field of tendency with multiple release
paths; **name ≥2.** Collapsing to a single Z is the H-read failure. (Direct fix for the Fed
read in §4: the regime-change disposition had a *rebound* release path the chain ignored.)

**Rule B — Rely on propensity; do not impose representation.** Never carry a represented
template — a prior year's event, a trained-in analogue, a modal completion — as a present
fact. Read *this* configuration's lean. (Structural cure for the 037–039 cross-year
projection, deeper than factual verification.)

## 4. Pilot calibration — the 058→059→060 chain

The cleanest available test set, because the reads in Briefing 059 resolved within 48 hours in
Briefing 060. This demonstrates the lens before the full sweep applies it to ~150 chains.

| Read (from B058/B059) | Read-mode | Resolved in B060 | Outcome | Shi note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Signed Iran MoU → **oil premium compresses** | **O** | CENTCOM ended blockade, Hormuz reopened, oil → ~$76 | **Held** | Clean orienting win: configuration (war-premium-in-price) + lean (signing drains it) + ripeness (high). |
| Lebanon clause hardens → **Conditional Collapse**, talks can't get past it | **O** | Ceasefire-and-airstrike same day; nuclear talks **postponed** | **Held-at-Y** (Z "premium snaps back" pending) | Strong propensity read; the Z over-reached on a single path — the field also allowed "talks stall, oil stays low," which is what occurred. |
| Pre-release access order **normalizes into a standing regime**, binds unevenly | **O** | Escalated to an **export-control recall** + EU-propagating precedent | **Held-direction / Velocity (Type-V)** | Under-read **ripeness** (move 3): the propensity was nearer release than "normalizes" implied — it snapped to a recall, not a gradual regime. |
| Continuity Mispricing → **market reprices down** (Fed regime change) | **H** | 17 Jun selloff (−1.21%) **then** 18 Jun rebound (+1.08%), path unchanged | **Held-in-window / Failed-on-path** | The hybrid: configuration (regime change) read correctly; Z collapsed to a single vector "down." Violated **Rule A** — the rebound was an unnamed release path. *This is where we were "off."* |
| Hegseth review → Europe treats guarantee as variable, accelerates independent capability | **O** | Review reverberating; European recalculation underway | **Held-at-Y** (longer Z pending) | Orienting read; transformation (move 4) correctly tracked the alliance configuration shifting. |

**Headline finding of the pilot:** *Read-mode tracked break severity exactly as hypothesized.*
The four **O-reads** held or degraded gently (Held / Held-at-Y / Velocity). The single **H-read**
— the one that collapsed a live disposition into a single vector — is precisely where the
briefing was wrong, and the error was not in the structure (the regime change was real) but in
the **vector commitment** (down-and-stays). The two concrete skills the pilot indicts are
**ripeness (move 3)** and **field-not-vector (Rule A)** — not the configurational reading,
which was sound throughout.

This is the pragmatic payoff: the discipline does not ask the briefing to read *better
structures*; it asks it to **stop converting good configurational reads into bad single-vector
predictions at the last step.** That is the bandwidth-rotation insight operationalized — the
trap is the over-commitment to representation, not the orientation.

## 5. What this feeds

**Into daily practice (recalibrations for Briefings 061–090):**
1. Every Inference Engine chain tags its read-mode (O/R/H) at creation; H and R chains must
   either be rewritten as O or carry an explicit justification.
2. Every chain names **≥2 release paths** at the Z-step (Rule A) — verifiable by grep.
3. Every "untested instrument"-style read states its **ripeness** (move 3): near or far release.
4. The cross-year/template-projection check is reframed from a factual catch to a **read-mode
   catch**: any read carrying a prior-period analogue as present fact is an R-read and is
   rewritten (Rule B).

**Into the theory (orienting vs. knowledge representation):**
The briefing becomes the empirical engine. Each cycle yields a labeled corpus of O/R/H reads
with resolved outcomes — a dataset on *when representation pays and when orientation pays*
under graded dynamism. The prediction, straight from bandwidth-rotation: representation reads
win only in the low-flux tail (settled, fast-settling clauses — a strait either opens or not);
orientation reads win as flux rises (contested, slow-settling clauses — a nuclear schedule, a
Lebanon understanding). Briefing 060's own thread (the clause that executes vs. the clause
that stalls) is already this distinction in the world: the fast-settling clause is representable;
the slow-settling clause can only be oriented to.
